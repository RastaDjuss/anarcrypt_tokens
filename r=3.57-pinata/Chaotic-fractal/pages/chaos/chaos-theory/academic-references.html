Academic References on Chaos Theory and Classical Mechanics

Major Works and Syntheses

  • Aspects of Classical and Celestial Mechanics, Encyclopaedia of Mathematical Sciences, Springer-Verlag (2nd Edition, 1993).

    A synthesis on celestial mechanics and the classical theory of dynamical systems by Vladimir Arnold and his collaborators, considered as an advanced education reference (university level).

  • Vladimir Arnold and André Avez, Ergodic Problems of Classical Mechanics, Advanced Book Classics, Addison-Wesley, 1988.

    Re-edition of a fundamental work originally written in 1968, addressing ergodic properties of mechanical systems.

  • David Ruelle and Jean-Pierre Eckmann, "Ergodic Theory of Chaos and Strange Attractors", Review of Modern Physics, Vol. 57, 1985, pp. 617-656.

    An in-depth exploration of strange attractors and ergodic systems in chaos theory.

  • Vladimir Damgov, Nonlinear and Parametric Phenomena - Applications in Radiophysical and Mechanical Systems, World Scientific, Series on Nonlinear Sciences, 2004.

    Study on nonlinear parametric phenomena applied to physical systems.

  • René Lozi, "Can We Trust in Numerical Computations of Chaotic Solutions of Dynamical Systems?", in Topology and Dynamics of Chaos in Celebration of Robert Gilmore’s 70th Birthday, World Scientific Series on Nonlinear Science, Vol. 84, 2013, pp. 63-98.

    A critical analysis of the limitations of numerical calculations applied to chaotic systems.

Key Scientific Articles

  • Rössler Otto E. (1976), "A Equation for Continuous Chaos", Physics Letters A, No. 57, pp. 397-398.

    Introduction of the Rössler equation, one of the essential chaotic differential systems.

  • Li Tien-Yien & James Yorke (1975), "Period Three Implies Chaos", American Mathematical Monthly, Vol. 82, pp. 985-992.

    An accessible and fundamental proof in the study of nonlinear dynamical systems.

  • Edward N. Lorenz (1963), "Deterministic Non-periodic Flow", Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences, Vol. 20(2), pp. 130-141.

    The founding paper of the "deterministic chaos" concept, including the butterfly effect.

  • David Ruelle & Floris Takens (1971), "On the Nature of Turbulence", Communications in Mathematical Physics, No. 20, pp. 167-192.

    Study on turbulence through strange attractors and their role in nonlinear systems.

  • Ian Stewart (1991), "Deciding the Undecidable", Nature, Vol. 352, pp. 664–665.

    Discussion on the algebraic limits of chaotic systems.

Mathematical Complements on Chaos

  • Christophe Letellier, Chaos in Nature, Paris, Vuibert, 2006.

    Mathematical exploration of chaotic dynamics observed in natural systems.

  • Pierre-Simon Laplace (1814), Philosophical Essay on Probabilities.

    A foundational text linking differential systems to chaotic behavior and the limits of chance.

  • Henri Poincaré, Science and Method.

    Theoretical introduction to the fundamental concepts that led to the understanding of dynamical systems.
