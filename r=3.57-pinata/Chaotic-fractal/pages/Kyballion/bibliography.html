Bibliography of The Kybalion and Related Works

The Kybalion

Published in 1908 by The Three Initiates, The Kybalion remains one of the most influential spiritual works of the 20th century. It focuses on seven Hermetic principles:

  • Mentalism
  • Correspondence
  • Vibration
  • Polarity
  • Rhythm
  • Cause and Effect
  • Gender

Though its precise authorship remains uncertain, William Walker Atkinson is considered its most likely primary author.

Influential Hermetic Sources

The Kybalion draws heavily on earlier Hermetic works, such as:

  • The **Corpus Hermeticum** - A collection of ancient writings reflecting Hermetic philosophy.
  • The **Emerald Tablet** - A profound alchemical text attributed to Hermes Trismegistus.
  • Various Renaissance commentaries by scholars like Marsilio Ficino and Frances Yates.

Modern Interpretations and Studies

Scholars and authors continue to study The Kybalion and Hermeticism, producing works such as:

  • "The Light of Egypt" by Thomas Burgoyne
  • "Hermetica: The Greek Corpus Hermeticum and the Latin Asclepius" translated by Brian P. Copenhaver
  • "The Secret Doctrine" by Helena Blavatsky
